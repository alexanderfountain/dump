## FAQs for Middlesex County NJ Dumpster Rentals

### What can and can't be recycled in Middlesex County NJ?

The following items are considered recyclable by Middlesex County and are permitted for curbside pickup through the city recycling service:

- Aluminum cans
- Glass bottles and jars
- Steel cans
- Plastic Bottles with #1 code or #2 code found on the bottom
- Newspaper
- Mixed paper (magazines, office paper, junk mail, cereal boxes)
- Corrugated cardboard

In Middlesex County, the following items may be recycled but are NOT subject to curbside pickup. The
<a href='http://www.middlesexcountynj.gov/Government/Departments/PSH/Pages/General-Recycling.aspx' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Middlesex County website</a> states that each municipality may have separate curbside programs for these materials or be able to accept them at a specific drop-off center:

- Leaves
- Brush
- Motor oil
- <a href='http://www.middlesexcountynj.gov/Government/Departments/PSH/Pages/textiles.aspx' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Textiles​</a>
- Masonry/paving materials (asphalt, block, brick, cinder and concrete)
- Rechargeable batteries (Nickel Cadmium (Ni-Cd), Nickel Metal Hydride (Ni-MH), Lithium Ion (Li-
ion) and Small Sealed Lead (Pb))
- Tires
- Computers, televisions, computer monitors, printers and fax machines
- Electronics (washers, dryers, ranges, refrigerators, air conditioners)

### How can I dispose of hazardous waste and other restricted items in Middlesex County NJ?

Middlesex County has a robust hazardous waste recycling program offering residents about six different drop-off days a year. Check the <a href='http://www.middlesexcountynj.gov/Government/Departments/PSH/Pages/Household-Hazardous-Waste.aspx' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Middlesex County Hazardous Waste Page</a> for this year's days.

Materials that will be accepted at these events include: Adhesives, Aerosol Cans, Antifreeze, Brake Fluid, Car Batteries, Cleaners, Drain Cleaners, Fire Extinguishers, Flares, Fluorescent Bulbs, Gasoline, Gas/Oil Mix, Kerosene, Lighter Fluid, Mercury, Motor Oil/Filters, Paints, Paint Thinner, Pesticides and Herbicides, Photographic Chemicals, Pool Chemicals, Propane Tanks, Stains and Varnishes, Thermometers, and Thermostats.

A special note about rechargeable, button cell batteries, and asbestos: Rechargeable and button cell batteries are required to be individually bagged and/or have their terminal ends individually taped up. Alkaline batteries are permitted to be disposed of in your normal trash. In terms of asbestos, you must call 732-745-4170 in advance if you are planning on disposing of it.

Items that will not be accepted include: Appliances, Furniture, Electronics, Empty Containers, Explosives and Munitions, Infectious or Medical Waste, Smoke Detectors, and Tires.
