### Step Three: Dumpster permit regulations

Now that you’ve chosen the perfect size, it’s time to determine whether or not you’ll need to obtain a permit. The process in Mount Laurel is very simple. You do not need a permit if you are placing the dumpster on your own property, such as a driveway. However, dumpsters can never be placed on a public right-of-way so as to interfere with the flow of traffic. All other instances need approval from the township. We recommend you call your city’s public works office at 856-234-0001 if you have any questions regarding where your dumpster can be placed.

After ironing out all the details, you’re now read to order your dumpster! Our transparent pricing model is based on three factors: weight, location, and type of debris. Once you have purchased your dumpster rental and scheduled its drop off, the final step is to clear the drop off location of any obstacles so that our roll off truck can quickly place your dumpster in the desired location. ## Recycling Guide for Mount Laurel NJ

The following items can be recycled: Newspaper, cardboard, magazines, paperback books, phone books, computer paper, office and school paper, gray color cardboard (cereal boxes). Glass bottles, glass food containers, aluminum cans, steel cans, plastic bottles #1 or #2, empty aerosol cans.

The following items cannot be recycled: Pizza boxes, hardback books, large amounts of shredded paper, soiled paper/cardboard, food contaminated cardboard/paper, foil or plastic coated paper/cardboard, clay or plastic flower pots, crystal, aluminum foil, plastic bags, plastic items that are not a bottle, plastic bottles #3, #4,#6, and #7, ceramic mugs or plates, drinking glasses, ovenware, light bulbs, mirrors, window glass, motor oil or pesticide containers.

Recyclables are picked up in Mount Laurel every other Tuesday at 6 am. If you have additional questions regarding what can be recycled, more information can be found on the <a href="http://www.co.burlington.nj.us/DocumentCenter/View/3166/125RecyclingItemsREV0315?bidId" target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Burlington County Recycling Guide</a>.

## Disposing of Hazardous Material in Mount Laurel NJ

Mount Laurel holds a hazardous material drop-off on the first and third Saturday of every month (unless it is a holiday weekend at which it will be pushed to the following weekend) at the recycling center. The drop off runs from 8 am to 12 pm, and you must show proof of residency when dropping items off.

The following items should be dropped off as hazardous material: Paint(latex and oil based), solvents, driveway sealers, aerosols, antifreeze, oil, batteries, fluorescent tubes and ballasts, pesticides, pool chemicals and gasoline (which should be brought in a clearly marked container). You can find more information on Mount Laurel’s recycling and hazardous waste collection efforts on the <a href="https://www.mountlaurel.com/departments/public_works_department/recycling/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Mount Laural Township website</a>.
