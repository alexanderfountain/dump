## Your Randolph NJ Recycling Guide

As we are a company that takes pride in our sustainability efforts, we like to encourage our customers and the general public to recycle. Not only is recycling good for the environment, but it also makes the area look a lot better. Recycling throughout the state of New Jersey is the same throughout with some key differences regarding the processes. In Randolph, New Jersey, there are two methods used in recycling; a centrally located recycling center and a bimonthly curbside pickup service. Here is some information about recycling for residents of Randolph, New Jersey.

Recycling Center: The recycling center is limited to residents of Randolph, New Jersey only. To enter, you need proof of residency as well as a sticker provided to you for free by the center. There are some materials not allowed to be brought in, such as hazardous gasoline, pool chemicals, and biohazardous waste. Aside from the typical recyclables paper, plastic, and glass, other items accepted by the recycling center include:
- Tires
- Batteries
- Branches
- Clothing, toys, household goods
- Electronic waste

Curbside Pickup: Recyclables are picked up every other week. All recyclable items must be placed curbside by 6:00 am. Collection day is determined by your street, visit <a href='https://www.randolphnj.org/services/collection_routes' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Randolph's website to find your route</a>. And instead of separating the recyclables, they all must be placed together in one recycling bin. This includes:
- Plastic containers
- Aluminum and metal cans
- Glass bottles and jars
- Newspapers, magazines, and all paper goods
- Cardboard

So, if you have debris such as branches and waste that may not fall into the requirements for curbside pickup, residents of Randolph are able to take said materials to the recycling center instead. For more information visit <a href='https://www.randolphnj.org/services/recycling_information' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Randolph Township's New Jersey recycling page</a>.
