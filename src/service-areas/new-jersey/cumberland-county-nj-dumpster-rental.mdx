## Cumberland County Recycling

While it is required by state law to recycle, Cumberland County goes above and beyond. The residents, organizations and businesses have made Cumberland County the leader in recycling for the State of New Jersey. NJDEP statistics report that Cumberland County has been <a href='https://www.ccia-net.com/recycling/' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">number one in the state for seven years in a row</a> from 2010 to 2017. Cumberland County residents are able to take advantage of single stream recycling and combine all recycling materials into one container!

Cumberland County’s DO Recycle List:

- Office paper
- Envelopes (with or without windows)
- Cereal boxes
- Junk mail
- Magazines/Newspapers
- Telephone books
- Cardboard (corrugated must be flattened)
- Wrapping paper/tissue paper
- Paperback
- #1 & #2 plastics
- Glass bottles/containers/jars
- Metals, Aluminum/Steel/Tin Cans
- Empty non-hazardous aerosol cans (removed lid)

Cumberland County’s DON’T Recycle List:

- Plastic bags
- Shredded paper
- Hazardous Waste
- Styrofoam
- Liquids in bottles or containers
- China, dishware, glassware, or vases
- Aluminum foil
- Paper or plastic plates, towels & utensils
- Rigid plastics, toys, chairs
- Plastic containers without a number
- Broken glass or mirrors
- Light bulbs
- Batteries
- Wire clothes hangers
- Pots and pans
- Tire rims or car parts
- Food waste or garbage
- Syringes, needles, or sharps
- Plastic #3, #4, #5, #6, or #7

Cumberland County offers drop off centers for residents to bring their electronics, used oil, and antifreeze.
