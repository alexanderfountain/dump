## FAQs for Essex County Dumpster Rental Service

### What can and can't be thrown in a dumpster in Essex County NJ?

Typically, any non-hazardous material can be put into a dumpster. Most household waste, bulky
furniture, construction debris, yard waste and appliances are perfectly acceptable. However, there are certain debris that should never be thrown in a dumpster whether you’re in Essex county or anywhere else in the United States. This includes asbestos, wet paint, electronics, tires, batteries, fuel, oil, propane tanks, hot water tanks, and other hazardous waste. Our guide <Link to={Paths.guideWhatCanGoIntoDumpster}>What Can You Throw in a Dumpster</Link> outlines these regulations in more detail and answers more of the specific questions we have received regarding this. If you are still unsure, don’t hesitate to <Link to={Paths.contact}>contact us</Link> with any questions!

### How can I dispose of hazardous waste and other restricted items in Essex County NJ?

Essex County has a robust program for helping residents dispose of materials that are not allowed in a dumpster. Thanks to the <a href='https://www.nj.gov/dep/' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">NJ Department of Environmental Protection</a>, the County is able to provide two Household Hazardous Waste Collection Days, in May and October. These are both held at the Essex County Fleet Maintenance Facility at 99 West Bradford Avenue in Cedar Grove. Refer to the <a href='http://www.ecuanj.com/newsevents.html' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Essex County Utility Authority’s Events</a> page for specific dates. A comprehensive compilation of helpful links and FAQ answers can be found on their Solid Waste and Recycling Page as well.

### Do I need a permit to rent a dumpster in Essex County NJ?

For Essex County NJ, permit requirements depend on where the dumpster rental will be placed. If you
are planning to place the dumpster on your driveway or anywhere else on your personal property, you
will generally not need a permit to rent a dumpster in Essex County. However, if you are placing the
dumpster on public property, like a county road, you will need to apply and pay for a permit through the <a href='http://ecdpw.org/permits_application.php' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Essex County Department of Public Works</a>. Dumpster permit requirements can change from town to town, to get a better idea of the specific regulations search for your town on our <Link to={Paths.dumpsterPermits}>dumpster rental permits</Link> page.
