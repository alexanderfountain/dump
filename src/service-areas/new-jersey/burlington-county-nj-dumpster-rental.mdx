### What can and cannot be thrown away in a dumpster in Burlington County NJ?

Generally, any non-hazardous waste can be thrown away in a dumpster. This includes most household waste, bulky furniture, construction debris, yard waste, and appliances. However, there are certain materials that are considered hazardous waste and cannot be thrown away in a dumpster anywhere in the United States, including Burlington County. Items such as asbestos, wet paint, electronics, tires, batteries, fuel, oil, propane tanks, and hot water tanks are considered hazardous waste. To learn more about these regulations, you can read our <Link to={Paths.guideWhatCanGoIntoDumpster}>What Can You Throw in a Dumpster</Link> guide which answers the more specific questions we have received regarding this. ### How do I dispose of hazardous waste in Burlington County NJ?

If you need to dispose of any hazardous waste, Burlington County has made it easy to do so. The county has a dedicated hazardous waste collection facility that is available free of charge to residents and small businesses in Burlington County. The facility is open 6 days a week, no appointment is necessary, and all materials will be accepted free of charge, just provide proof of residency. ## Burlington County Recycling Guide

Burlington County provides its residents with curbside recycling. The following items can be placed in your recycling container to avoid sending them to the landfill:

- Paper
- Flattened cardboard
- Plastic bottles and food containers
- Glass bottles and jars
- Metal cans
- Cartons

The following items cannot be recycled:

- Plastic bags
- Plastic wrap or newspaper sleeves
- Tanglers – hoses, cords or wire hangers
- Plastic plant pots, pill bottles or flat plastic (like trays and lids)
- Aluminum foil or trays
- Hardback books, food soiled or frozen food boxes
- Bulbs, glassware or window glass
- Motor oil, paint, pesticide containers or propane tanks
- Plastics #3, #4, #6, or #7
- Styrofoam
- Loose caps or any hot or cold drinking cups/straws
- Batteries

Burlington County also recommends the following recycling tips, which can be found in their <a href="https://www.co.burlington.nj.us/DocumentCenter/View/4628/NonCart-Inside-Panel-Final-May-2016?bidId" target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">recycling bin guide</a>.

- No shredded paper (shredded paper is too small to sort; it just jams the equipment and ultimately ends up in the trash)
- Flatten boxes
- Remove and throw away Styrofoam and plastic wrap
- Empty and rinse all containers
- Do NOT flatten aluminum cans
- No need to remove any labels
- Your municipal recycling drop off is available for overflow

Please note that the above information and recycling guide is for households with recycling bins. Some residencies in Burlington County may have recycling carts instead. For more recycling information for carts, please visit the <a href="https://www.co.burlington.nj.us/DocumentCenter/View/4619/Residential-Cart-Guidelines?bidId" target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">recycling cart guide”</a>.
