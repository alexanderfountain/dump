## Edison New Jersey Recycling Guide

At Alliance, we take go to lengths to make sure as much of your waste is recycled as possible. As an Edison Township resident, we encourage you to do the same and make sure you’re doing your part to give back to the planet. The first step in becoming a more educated recycler is understanding what can and can’t be recycled in Edison.

Accepted Recyclables:

- Plastic with #1 or #2 Symbols
- Mixed Paper (paper, newspaper, magazines, soft cover books)
- Cardboard (corrugated, cereal boxes, clean pizza boxes)
- Aluminum and Tin Cans
- Glass Bottles and Jars

Not Recyclable:

- Shredded Paper
- Plastic Bags
- Greasy/Food Contaminated Items
- Styrofoam
- Plastic #3 – #7
- Pots, Pans, and Small Appliances
- Syringes, Batteries, and Bulbs
- Dishware, Glass, and Mirrors
- Garden Hoses and Electric Cords

Edison Township Recycling Service

Edison offers their residents a bi-weekly curbside recycling pickup service in which the acceptable recyclables listed above will be disposed of for you. Your scheduled pickup is determined by which section of Edison your residence falls within. Edison Public Works has divided the town into 10 sub-sections and has allocated each section a pickup day. To view your section and determine when your recycling will be picked up, visit the Edison Public Works <a href='https://www.edisonnj.org/departments/public_works/index.php' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Recycling & Sanitation Calendar</a>. When using this service residents must fully separate garbage from recycling, use the provided recycling receptacle, and place any cardboard that is unable to fit inside the bins next to them.

Bulk Items

Getting rid of bulk items like branches and construction debris can be a taxing experience to say the least. Edison Township offers bulk pickup curbside for select items, but you must schedule it with them and wait to see when they’ll be able to pick everything up. Some items like leaves and fencing are picked up during the fall and spring, but only a limited number of times per household. All in all, the bulk item pickup schedule is fairly confusing and requires some deep digging to figure out exactly what can and can’t be picked up. If you’d like to find out everything Edison has to offer, check out their comprehensive <a href='https://www.edisonnj.org/departments/public_works/index.php' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Recycling & Sanitation Calendar</a>.
