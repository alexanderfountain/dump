## Recycling Guide for Long Branch NJ

Long Branch picks up commingled recyclables. However, cardboard and paper products, such as newspapers, must be separated. All cardboard and newspapers must be collapsed and flattened into bundles of not more than 50 lbs and tied. No more than 8 bundles of cardboard can be placed on it's pickup day. The city has also issued a notice asking residents to please refrain from placing commingled recyclable items in plastic bags, as these bags cause the machines at recycling plants to jam. Please place all commingle items in a reusable container. For more information on recycling, please visit the <a href="https://www.longbranch.org/departments/PublicWorks/garbage-recycling-information" target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">City of Long Branch’s website</a>.

## Disposing of Hazardous Waste in Long Branch NJ

Long Branch has the same regulations on hazardous waste as its county, Monmouth County. All hazardous waste (asbestos, wet paint, electronics, tires, batteries, fuel, oil, propane tanks, hot water tanks, etc.) should be dropped off at the <a href="https://www.visitmonmouth.com/page.aspx?ID=2258" target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Monmouth County Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) Facility</a>, and not be placed into dumpsters. For more information on what is considered hazardous waste, please visit our <Link to={Paths.guideWhatCanGoIntoDumpster}>What Can You Throw in a Dumpster</Link> guide.
