### Check for possible permits

To rent a roll off dumpster in Pennsville, a permit is not required as long as it is placed in your driveway. Though if you need to keep your dumpster rental on the street, you will need to contact Pennsville Township’s clerk’s office, (856) 678-3089 ext. 124, and fill out a <a href='https://www.pennsville.org/download/forms_library/clerks-office/DUMPSTER%20%20PERMIT%20APPLICATION.pdf' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">permit form</a> to get approval to place a dumpster in the street. There is a $100 deposit that is refundable if you have the dumpster removed within 10 days. Read about all of <a href='https://www.pennsville.org/download/forms_library/clerks-office/Dupster%20Permit%20Rules%20and%20Regulations.pdf' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Pennsville's dumpster rental permit regulations</a> before renting your dumpster if you plan to have it in the street.

## Recycling in Pennsville Township

Recycling is picked up every other week the same day trash is picked up. Another option the residents of Pennsville have is to bring their recyclables to the Industrial Road Complex. For more information on recycling and trash services in Pennsville Township, New Jersey, check out their <a href='https://www.pennsville.org/about-us/community-information/trash_recycling/' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">trash and recycling guide</a>.

Pennsville also uses the application Recycle Coach to handle its residents recycling needs. Recycle Coach helps make the waste management process easy and more effective. The application provides users with trash and recycling collection days and times based on their address, guides on how to recycle different items, and real time notifications in the case of any service disruptions. Check out their website for more information about <a href='https://www.pennsville.org/recycle-coach/' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Recycle Coach in the Pennsville area</a>. Alliance Disposal recommends all residents of Pennsville to utilize this application because it encourages a better, greener environment, and we ourselves put effort towards a more sustainable future.

### Accepted Recyclable Items:

- Newspaper and Paper
- Cardboard (no wax)
- Only plastic bottles labeled #1 and #2 on the bottom
- Glass bottles and jars any color
- Aluminum and Tin cans

### Items Not Accepted:

- Aerosol cans
- Mirrors
- Window or auto glass
- Light bulbs
- Ceramics
- Oil or antifreeze containers
- Plastic bags
- Coat hangers

The full list of items can be found on the <a href='https://www.pennsville.org/about-us/community-information/trash_recycling/' target="_blank" rel="noopener norefferer">Pennsville recycling page</a>.

### The following items will not be taken care of by Pennsville Township either:

- Concrete
- Bricks
- Tree trunks
- Very large tree limbs
- Brush put out by contractors
- Hazardous waste

To deal with these items, the township recommends they be taken to the Salem county Improvement Authority. In addition, hazardous waste can also be collected separately. The Salem County Improvement Authority holds a Household Hazardous Waste Collection Day twice a year. Alliance Disposal can also help you remove these items, simply <Link to={Paths.contact}>contact us</Link>.
